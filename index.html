About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Operations Management area at Booth School of Business, University of Chicago, and I work with Prof. Ozan Candogan and Prof. Rad Niazadeh. I received my Ph.D. in Decision Sciences from Fuqua School of Business, Duke University, under the supervision of Prof. David Brown and Prof. Santiago Balseiro. I spent the summer of 2019 as a data scientist intern at Uber's Marketplace Optimization group, working on matching problems.

Research Interests

My research is centered on improving the operations of modern marketplaces by developing efficient data-driven policies that are easy to implement and interpret and attain good performance guarantees. Such policies are not only valued in practice, but also provide critical insights into the operational problems we study.

On the practical side, I have followed such research guidelines to address various operational challenges in online platforms and markets, including: (i) optimal design of experiments to collect information efficiently, (ii) dynamic pricing for ride-sharing platforms, and (iii) efficient algorithms for stochastic job scheduling.
